Full Stack AI Governance Presented at UCAAT 2025

AI Documentation Platform for EU AI Act Compliance

MERN Stack · TypeScript · AdminJS · RBAC · MongoDB Atlas

When the EU AI Act came into force, it introduced Article 11 — a legal obligation requiring high-risk AI systems to maintain rigorous technical documentation across their entire lifecycle. To replace unstructured spreadsheets and fragmented PDFs, I engineered a unified compliance environment as part of my Master's work at the University of Göttingen.

The platform provides a structured, role-aware architecture for tracking AI systems from training through post-market audit. It supports dynamic regulatory templates, a fine-grained Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) layer supporting Admins, Contributors, and Auditors, and a mathematical scoring mechanism that assesses documentation transparency.

I validated the implementation against three CE-certified medical AI devices operating in live clinical environments: Vara (mammography screening), Annalise (chest imaging), and Us2.ai (echocardiography)—environments where system transparency is fundamentally linked to patient outcomes.

Selected and peer-reviewed for UCAAT 2025 — the international workshop on user-centric security and privacy in Europe. This project stands as one of the first production-ready implementations of an Article 11 compliance pipeline tested against production clinical AI.

Computer Vision Applied AI Developed at HerHackathon

CareForSkin — Skin Condition Detection & Grounded RAG Pipeline

Python · Streamlit · Roboflow · OpenCV · RAG · LLMs

The digital health and skincare space is flooded with anecdotal advice and unvalidated self-diagnosis tools. I wanted to design an application that replaces user guesswork with direct computer vision inference paired with highly contextual, specialized language models.

Developed during HerHackathon—a prominent European hackathon championing women in tech—CareForSkin processes user image uploads, pipes them through a customized Roboflow object-detection model trained on dermatological features, and draws boundary boxes on identified conditions.

To ensure safety, the advice layer utilizes a **Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)** pipeline. Instead of relying on raw LLM outputs prone to hallucination, the model queries an isolated, peer-reviewed clinical knowledge base to serve secure, highly-grounded skin health recommendations.

Infrastructure DevSecOps GWDG Mentored

HashiCorp Vault — Zero-Trust Secrets Lifecycle Management

HashiCorp Vault · PKI Infrastructure · Dynamic Credentials · HCL Policy

Hardcoded tokens, environment files tracked in version control, and static API keys represent systemic single points of failure. Under the **mentorship of engineers at GWDG** (the scientific data processing center for the University of Göttingen and the Max Planck Society), I designed a hardened secrets infrastructure based on zero-trust principles.

The architectural deployment establishes HashiCorp Vault as an absolute root of trust. I structured dynamic engine configurations that provision short-lived, automated credentials per-request, mitigating the risk of static credential leakage. Additionally, I deployed an internal Public Key Infrastructure (PKI) to generate TLS certificates on the fly and authored least-privilege access rules using HashiCorp Configuration Language (HCL).

This implementation emphasizes security as a structural architecture rather than an after-the-fact addition. It reflects standard corporate patterns expected across highly regulated data sectors in Germany.

Data Engineering Architecture

Data Lakehouses — Transaction Logs & Storage Evolution

Delta Lake · Apache Iceberg · Parquet · ACID Transactions · Data Governance

Modern data engineering often forces an architectural compromise between the transactional integrity of data warehouses and the low-cost, multi-structured scalability of object-storage data lakes.

This deep technical analysis investigates the mechanics of modern Lakehouse layers, specifically parsing **Delta Lake’s ACID-compliant transaction logs** and **Apache Iceberg’s manifest-driven metadata structures**. Guided by infrastructure mentors, this research evaluates partition-free indexing, time-travel queries, and schema-enforcement states, isolating the exact operational boundaries where a lakehouse outperforms legacy enterprise architectures.
